Recipes

Spandauer with cream and jam

Ingredients

Dough

2½ dl milk with a maximum of 0.7% fat

1 hp yeast (50 g)

1 egg

50 g liquid margarine

2 tbsp. sugar

¼ tsp. salt

150 g oatmeal, finely rolled (5 dl)

ca. 350 g wheat flour (approx. 5½ dl)

Cream

1 egg

1 tbsp. corn starch

2 parts milk with a maximum of 0.7% fat

1 tsp. vanilla sugar

sweetener equivalent to 20-25 g of sugar, preferably liquid

Fill

100 g of jam without added sugar eg. Strawberry

Garnish

Glaze possibly. colored, melted chocolate or icing

Serving size

16 pcs.

Method

Cream (cook this first)

Whisk eggs, cornstarch, milk and vanilla sugar together in a thick-bottomed pan. Bring the mixture to a boil and cook for 1-2 minutes while whipping.

Add sweetener, taste and cool cream, covered, in refrigerator.

Dough

Heat the milk until hand-warm and dissolve the yeast in it. Add eggs, margarine, sugar, salt, oatmeal and most of the wheat flour

Knead the dough well and add any. the last wheat flour until the dough is released.

Let the dough rest for approx. 5 min.

Divide the dough into 16 equal parts and roll it into buns. Press or roll with a rolling pin buns flat (about 10 cm in diameter) and dispense 1 tbsp. cream and 1 tsp. jam on each.

Fold the dough together against the filling and place the buns on a baking sheet with baking paper. Allow the buns to rise for approx. 15 min.

Bake them at 200⁰ C for approx. 15 min. on the middle or second upper furnace groove. Cool them on a baking rack.

If you want to decorate the lobster balls, you can melt approx. 25 g of chocolate in a small plastic microwave oven bag. Cut a very small corner of the bag and sprinkle the chocolate on the buns.

You can also make icing of icing and boiling water and decorate it. Color the glaze with fruit color. Alternatively, sprinkle icing over the buns.

Variation

Replace jam with 50 g of dark or light chocolate. You can also use raisins to fill with the cream

Tip

You can make the cream the day before you bake pie buns. Store in a refrigerator.

Nutrition Facts
